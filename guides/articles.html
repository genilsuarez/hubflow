Articles a / an / the / ∅

When to use which article — a visual decision system.

a/an
Indefinite
One of many. First mention. Not specific.
the
Definite
We both know which one. Already mentioned.
∅
Zero Article
Uncountable/plural general. Abstract ideas.
a vs an
Sound Rule
Use "an" before vowel SOUNDS, not letters.

🔵 a / an — Indefinite

✅ Use a/an when...
First mention, one of many, jobs, describing what something is.
I saw a dog. She's an engineer. It's a beautiful day. Once a week.
🔈 a vs an = SOUND, not spelling
Use an before vowel sounds. Use a before consonant sounds.
an hour (/aʊər/) a university (/juːnɪ/) an MBA a European an honest person

🟢 the — Definite

✅ Use "the" when...
Both speaker and listener know which one: already mentioned, only one exists, defined by context or clause.
I saw a dog. The dog was big. the sun / the moon the book you gave me the best restaurant
🌎 Geographic rules for "the"
Some place names use "the" — follow these patterns:
Use THENo article ∅
the United States, the UKFrance, Japan, Spain
the Amazon, the ThamesLake Titicaca, Mount Everest
the Pacific Ocean, the SaharaEurope, Asia, Africa
the Alps, the AndesBroadway, Oxford Street
the Hilton, the White HouseHeathrow Airport, Harvard

🟣 Zero Article ∅

✅ No article when...
General plural, uncountable abstracts, meals, sports, languages, most countries.
∅ Dogs are loyal. ∅ Love is blind. ∅ Breakfast is ready. She plays ∅ tennis. He speaks ∅ Spanish.

🚨 Common Mistakes

❌ Don't use "the" with...
the life is short Life is short. ✓
I go to the school I go to school. ✓
the people are kind People are kind. ✓
⚠️ Fixed expressions (memorize!)
at home / at work / at school by bus / by car / on foot go to bed / go to prison in hospital (BrE) / in the hospital (AmE)

🚫 Never Use "The"

✅ Categories that always take ∅ (no article)
Names • continents • most countries • towns/cities • streets • individual lakes/mountains • sports • languages • meals • illnesses • academic subjects • days/holidays • title + name • US states/UK counties
∅ Sarah is my best friend. She has travelled across ∅ Africa. ∅ Tokyo is my favourite city. He lives on ∅ Baker Street. ∅ Lake Como is in Italy. ∅ President Biden gave a speech. He's from ∅ Texas.
⚠️ The exceptions that DO take "the"
Plural family surnames, a few special place names, and using "the" for emphasis before a name.
We invited the Hammonds to the party. (= the Hammond family) She works in The Hague. (special exception) Do you mean THE Andy who lives down the road? (emphasis/distinguishing)
