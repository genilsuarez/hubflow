Modal Verbs Untangled

Can, could, must, might, should, shall, will — nine small words that carry huge shades of meaning. Here's how to pick the right one.

🎯 Ability, Permission & Possibility

✅ can / could
Can = present ability/permission. Could = past ability, OR a polite/possible version of "can".
She can speak French. Could you pass the salt? I could swim at age five.
🤔 may / might
May = formal permission or possibility. Might = weaker, more uncertain possibility.
You may leave now. It might rain later.

⚖️ Obligation & Rules

✅ must — strong obligation
A rule or an obligation the speaker feels personally (internal), or a confident deduction.
You must wear a seatbelt. She must be tired. (deduction)
❌ mustn't vs don't have to
Mustn't = forbidden. Don't have to = no obligation, but allowed. These are opposites, not synonyms!
You mustn't smoke here. (forbidden) You don't have to come. (optional)
👤 have to — external obligation
An obligation imposed by someone/something else (a rule, a boss, a law) — not the speaker's own feeling.
I have to finish this by 5pm. (my boss said so)
💡 should — advice, not obligation
Weaker than must/have to. A recommendation, not a rule.
You should see a dentist.

💬 Offers, Suggestions & Future

🤝 shall — offers & suggestions
Used with I/we to offer or suggest something. Rare with other subjects.
Shall we order dessert? Shall I open the window?
✅ will — certainty, promises
Future facts, promises, and spontaneous decisions.
I will call you tonight. It'll be fine, I promise.

📋 Be Supposed To

Expectation vs reality
Be supposed to describes what's expected/planned — often used when reality doesn't match the plan.
The meeting was supposed to start at 9. (but it didn't) You're not supposed to eat here. (against the rules)
