Prefer & Would Rather

Three ways to talk about preferences — each with its own grammar and its own level of formality.

💭 Which Expression?

General habit
prefer
An ongoing, general preference
I prefer tea to coffee.
Specific occasion
would prefer
A choice for right now, more formal
I'd prefer pizza tonight.
Specific, informal
would rather
A specific choice, casual tone
I'd rather stay in.

🧩 Structure

💡 What comes after each one
prefer + noun/-ing + to + noun/-ing
would prefer + noun/to-infinitive
would rather + base verb (no "to"!) + than + base verb
I prefer dogs to cats. I'd prefer to stay in tonight. I'd rather walk than drive.
⚠️ Formal alternatives
Prefer that + clause is a formal way to express a preference about what someone else should do.
We prefer that guests remove their shoes.
