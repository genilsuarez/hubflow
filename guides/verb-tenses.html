PRESENT Present Tenses

Present Simple S + V(s/es)
  • Facts, habits, routines
  • General truths
  • Timetables & schedules
alwaysusuallyoften every dayneversometimes
She works at a hospital. / Water boils at 100°C.
Present Continuous S + am/is/are + V-ing
  • Actions happening right now
  • Temporary situations
  • Future arrangements
nowright nowat the moment currentlytodaythis week
She is working from home today. / I 'm meeting John tomorrow.

PAST Past Tenses

Past Simple S + V-ed / V2
  • Completed actions at a specific past time
  • Sequences of past events
  • Past habits (used to)
yesterdaylast weekin 2020 agowhen I was...
She worked there for 5 years. / I saw him yesterday.
Past Continuous S + was/were + V-ing
  • Action in progress at a past moment
  • Background action interrupted by another
  • Two parallel past actions
whilewhenat that time as
I was sleeping when the phone rang. / While she was cooking, he was reading.

FUTURE Future Tenses

Future Simple (will) S + will + V
  • Spontaneous decisions
  • Predictions (opinion-based)
  • Promises, offers, requests
tomorrowI think...probably next yearin the future
I will help you. / It will rain tomorrow, I think.
Going to S + am/is/are + going to + V
  • Plans & intentions (decided before)
  • Predictions based on evidence
tonightthis weekendsoon Look! (evidence)
I 'm going to study medicine. / Look at those clouds — it 's going to rain.

PERFECT Perfect Tenses

Present Perfect S + have/has + V3 (past participle)
  • Past action with present relevance
  • Life experience (ever/never)
  • Unfinished time (today, this week)
  • Duration with for/since
everneveralready yetjustsince forso far
I have visited Paris twice. / She has lived here since 2019.
Past Perfect S + had + V3
  • Action completed BEFORE another past action
  • "The past of the past"
beforeafterby the time alreadywhen (+ past simple)
When I arrived, she had already left. / I had never seen snow before that trip.
