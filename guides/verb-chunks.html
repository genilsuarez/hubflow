Verb + Prep Chunks

Some verbs always need a specific preposition — learn them as one fixed chunk, not two separate words.

FIXED Verb + Preposition Chunks

Grouped by preposition — the verb and preposition always travel together.

to
listen to (escuchar)
talk to (hablar con)
belong to (pertenecer a)
at / for / after
look at (mirar)
look for (buscar)
look after (cuidar de)
laugh at (reírse de)
for
wait for (esperar)
ask for (pedir)
pay for (pagar por)
apologize for (disculparse por)
on
work on (trabajar en)
depend on (depender de)
spend on (gastar en)
concentrate on (concentrarse en)
insist on (insistir en)
of
take care of (cuidar / encargarse)
consist of (consistir en)
with / in
agree with (estar de acuerdo con)
believe in (creer en)
about
think about (pensar en)
dream about (soñar con)
complain about (quejarse de)
from
suffer from (sufrir de)
Tip: when you learn a new verb, always learn its preposition at the same time — write them together as one chunk (depend on, not just depend) so you never have to guess later.

WH- Question Chunks (Stranded Preposition)

In everyday spoken English, the preposition moves to the end of the question instead of before the Wh-word.

QuestionPrepMeaning

What are you looking at?at¿A qué/en qué…?
Who are you laughing at?at¿De quién te ríes…?
What are you working on?on¿En qué…?
Where are you going to?to¿A dónde…?
Who are you thinking about?about¿Sobre quién…?
What do you think of this?of¿Qué opinas de…?
What did you open it with?with¿Con qué…?
Who did you get this from?from¿De quién…?
Where are you staying at?at¿En dónde…?
Tip: ending a question with a preposition (Who are you talking to?) is completely natural and standard in everyday English — it is not incorrect, despite what some traditional grammar rules say.
