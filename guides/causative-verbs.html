Causative Verbs

Have, get, make, let, help — five verbs for talking about who really does an action.

🛠️ Which Causative Verb?

VerbMeaningStructureExample
haveArrange a servicehave + person + base verbI had the mechanic check my brakes.
getPersuade someoneget + person + to-infinitiveShe got her brother to help.
makeForce (no choice)make + person + base verbThe teacher made us rewrite it.
letGive permissionlet + person + base verbMy parents let me stay out late.
helpAssisthelp + person + (to) base verbCan you help me carry this?
💡 Have vs Get — the subtle difference
Have is neutral (you simply arrange it). Get implies more effort — you had to convince or persuade someone.
I had the plumber fix the leak. (arranged) I got him to fix it eventually. (took persuading)

🧩 Passive Causative

💡 Someone did it for you
Have/get + object + past participle — used when someone else performs a service for you (often paid).
I had my car washed. She got her hair cut. We had the house painted.
